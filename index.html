Thai green prawn curry

Servings: 4-6

Prep time: 10 mins

Cook time: 15 mins

Total time: 25 mins

Ingredients

    1 tablespoon vegetable oil

    1 small yellow onion, thinly sliced

    ⅓ cup thinly sliced green onions, white and green parts, from 4-5 green onions

    2 cloves garlic, minced

    2½ tablespoons Thai green curry paste

    1 (400 ml) can coconut milk

    ¼ cup water

    2 tablespoons fish sauce

    1 tablespoon dark or light brown sugar

    300g prawns, peeled and deveined

    2 tablespoons lime juice

    ¼ cup chopped fresh coriander

Method

    1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.

    2. Add the yellow onion and cook, stirring frequently, until soft, about 3 minutes. If the onions start to brown, reduce the heat to medium.

    3. Add the green onions, garlic, and green curry paste, and cook, stirring frequently, for 2 minutes more.

    4. Add the coconut milk, water, fish sauce, and sugar and bring to a gentle boil.

    5. Add the prawns and cook, stirring frequently, until the prawns are pink and just cooked through, 3 to 4 minutes.

    6. Stir in the lime juice and sprinkle with the coriander. Taste and adjust seasoning, if necessary, and serve with jasmine rice.

Note: Feel free to substitute the lime juice for lemon juice if necessary.

Fluffy American-style pancakes

Servings: 4

Prep time: 10 mins

Cook time: 10 mins

Total time: 20 mins

Ingredients

    135g plain flour

    1 teaspoon baking powder

    ½ teaspoon salt

    2 tablespoons granulated or caster sugar

    130 ml milk

    1 large egg, lightly beaten

    2 tablespoons melted butter (allowed to cool slightly), plus extra for cooking

    Butter and golden syrup, to serve (optional)

Method

    1. Sift the flour, baking powder, salt and caster sugar into a large bowl. In a separate bowl or jug, lightly whisk together the milk and egg, then whisk in the melted butter.

    2. Pour the milk mixture into the flour mixture and, using a fork, beat until you have a smooth batter. Any lumps will soon disappear with a little mixing. Let the batter stand for a few minutes.

    3. Heat a non-stick frying pan over a medium heat and add a knob of butter. When it's melted, add a ladle of batter (or two if your frying pan is big enough to cook two pancakes at the same time). It will seem very thick but this is how it should be.

    4. Wait for about 3 minutes until the top of the pancake begins to bubble, and the edges begin to set. Flip it over and cook for another two minutes until both sides are golden brown and the pancake has risen to about 1cm thick. If the pancake is too dark, reduce the heat slightly for the next round.

    5. Repeat until all the batter is used up. You can keep the pancakes warm in a low oven, but they taste best fresh out the pan.

    6. Serve with a drizzle of golden syrup and extra butter, if you like.

Notes:

    If using self-raising flour instead of plain flour, be sure to reduce the amount of baking powder added to avoid bitter-tasting pancakes.

    Be careful to not overmix the batter. This makes the batter thin and runny and can lead to thin and chewy pancakes. Aim for a slightly lumpy batter than easily falls off a spoon (rather than running off).

Air fryer flatbread pizza

Servings: 4

Prep time: 5 mins

Cook time: 5 mins

Total time: 10 mins

Ingredients

    4 flatbreads

    ¼ cup pizza sauce

    250g shredded mozzarella

    Your choice of toppings

Method

    1. Preheat your air fryer to 200°C.

    2. Spread pizza sauce on the flatbread leaving a 1cm border.

    3. Add the mozzarella evenly over the sauce, then do the same with your toppings.

    4. Place the pizza in the air fryer basket and cook until the crust is golden brown and the cheese has melted. This should take about 5 mins.

    5. Eat and enjoy!
